Our Recycling Process

We follow a comprehensive and efficient process to ensure maximum material recovery and minimal environmental impact.

Collection

We collect recyclable materials from homes, businesses, and community drop-off points across the region.

Sorting

Materials are carefully sorted by type and quality using both automated systems and manual inspection.

Processing

Sorted materials are processed into raw materials that can be used to manufacture new products.

Manufacturing

Recycled materials are transformed into new products, completing the circular economy loop.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and recycling in general.

What materials does Ecorevive accept for recycling?

We accept a wide range of materials including paper, cardboard, plastics (#1-7), glass, aluminum, steel cans, and electronics. Some specialty items like batteries and light bulbs require special handling - please contact us for details on these materials.

How do I start recycling with Ecorevive?

Getting started is easy! For residential service, you can sign up online or call our customer service team. We'll provide you with collection bins and a pickup schedule. For businesses, we offer free consultations to design a customized recycling program.

What happens to the materials after they're collected?

Collected materials are transported to our state-of-the-art sorting facility where they're separated by type and quality. After sorting, materials are processed (cleaned, shredded, melted, etc.) and sold to manufacturers who use them to make new products.

How does recycling benefit the environment?

Recycling conserves natural resources, saves energy, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and decreases pollution. For example, recycling aluminum uses 95% less energy than producing new aluminum from raw materials. It also reduces the need for landfills and incineration.

Do you offer educational programs about recycling?

Yes! We offer free educational programs for schools, community groups, and businesses. Our programs include facility tours, classroom presentations, and hands-on workshops. We also provide educational materials and curriculum resources for teachers.
